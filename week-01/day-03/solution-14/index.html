The Shard

The shard is one of the most amazing structures in the world. The Shard held the title of Europe's tallest skyscraper with a height of almost 310 meters (1016 ft). The tapered tower has a glass facade consisting of some eleven thousand window panes. The approximate price of each of the ten apartments between the 53rd and 65th floors is £50m.

Plans for the new skyscraper were initially drawn up by the architectural firm of Broadway Malyan, and called for a circular 365 meters tall tower. These plans were soon scaled back and a new design was submitted, this time from the hands of the Italian architect Renzo Piano. The renowned architect designed a glass pyramid-shaped structure with a height of just over three hundred meters. The plans for the London Bridge Tower - as the building was initially called - caused an outcry from preservationists who considered the glass tower inappropriate for a historic neighborhood with mostly low-rise brick buildings. They claimed the tower would cut through the neighborhood like a shard of glass. The term "The Shard" came about due to the semblance of the building to a shard of glass stabbing out of the ground.

In late 2007, the gathering uncertainty in the global financial markets sparked concerns about the viability of the Shard. However, in January 2008, Sellar announced that it had secured funding from a consortium of Qatari investors, who had paid £150 million to secure an 80% stake in the project. The consortium included Qatar National Bank, QInvest, Qatari Islamic Bank and the Qatari property developer Barwa Real Estate, as well as Sellar Property. The deal involved a buyout of the Halabi and CLS Holdings stakes, and part of the Sellar Property stake.[32] The new owners promised to provide the first tranche of finance, allowing construction of the tower to begin. In 2009, the State of Qatar consolidated its ownership of London Bridge Quarter, including the Shard, through the purchase of the private Qatari investors' stakes. London Bridge Quarter is today jointly owned by the State of Qatar and Sellar Property.

Fun facts about The Shard

  • There are 72 habitable floors. A further 15 levels will make up the "spire". Six have the potential to be used, while another nine are exposed to the elements.
  • It has 44 working lifts.
  • It has a floor area of 110,000 m2 (1,200,000 sq ft)
  • Construction started on March 16, 2009
  • Practical completion of the building was achieved in November 2012.
  • The average lift speed is 6 metres per second
  • Construction workers found a fox near the top
  • The Shard is made up of 11,000 glass panels on the exterior
  • You can enjoy the view when in the restroom, 244m above London
  • The viewing platform is actually three floors
